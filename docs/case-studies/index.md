## Case Detail: Summary Previews

### Case 1: ASTA Color Value Discrepancy at Port

A 20 MT shipment of Premium paprika powder (contracted ASTA 160–200) tested ASTA 138 at destination port. Investigation revealed the issue was not actual quality failure but sample preparation differences between supplier and buyer laboratories. The resolution involved arbitration by a third-party accredited laboratory and revision of the sampling protocol in the purchase contract.

**ROOT CAUSE:** Inconsistent sample grinding procedure — the supplier's laboratory used finer grinding (300 μm), achieving higher extraction efficiency, while the buyer's lab used coarser grind (500 μm), producing lower ASTA readings.

**KEY LESSON:** Specify sample preparation method (particle size, extraction time) in the contract, not just the test method.

### Case 2: Caking and Clumping in Container Shipment

A 40' container of Superior paprika arrived with significant caking — approximately 60% of bags had hard clumps rendering the product unusable for the buyer's application. Investigation identified that the container experienced a 15°C temperature differential during ocean transit, causing moisture migration and condensation within the bags. The resolution involved an insurance claim under marine cargo insurance.

**ROOT CAUSE:** Moisture migration due to temperature gradient (container loaded in 10°C conditions, experienced 35°C in tropical transit zones). Moisture barrier was insufficient for the extreme temperature swing.

**KEY LESSON:** For tropical routes, use foil-lined barrier bags or vacuum packaging to prevent moisture migration regardless of initial moisture content.

### Case 3: Salmonella Suspect — Border Detention

A container of organic paprika powder was detained at US border after an initial screening test indicated possible Salmonella presence. The 10-day detention cost $4,200 in demurrage and storage fees. Follow-up testing at an FDA-accredited laboratory confirmed negative — the initial result was a false positive due to cross-contamination during the screening test.

**ROOT CAUSE:** False positive from screening immunoassay. The confirmatory culture method per FDA BAM Chapter 5 returned negative.

**KEY LESSON:** Include a clause in supply contracts specifying that confirmatory testing must be conducted at an ISO 17025 accredited laboratory using the reference method before any rejection decision.
