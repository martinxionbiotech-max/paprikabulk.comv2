# Sensory Evaluation — Standardized Organoleptic Assessment of Paprika

## 1. Purpose & Scope

Sensory evaluation provides a structured, documented assessment of the visual, olfactory, and gustatory characteristics of paprika samples. While instrumental methods (ASTA spectrophotometry, CIELAB colorimetry, HPLC) quantify specific chemical attributes, sensory evaluation captures the **subjective experience** that ultimately determines customer satisfaction.

**Scope:** All paprika grades and forms (powder, flakes, whole pods). Applicable to incoming inspection, production quality control, and finished product release.

**Reference Standards:**
- **ISO 4121:2003** — Sensory analysis — Methodology — Evaluation of food products by methods using scales
- **ISO 13299:2016** — Sensory analysis — General guidance for establishing a sensory profile
- **ASTM E1885-22** — Standard test method for sensory evaluation — Duo-trio test

---

## 2. Evaluation Environment

| Parameter | Requirement | Rationale |
|:---------:|:-----------:|:----------|
| Room temperature | 20–22°C | Avoid temperature adaptation effects |
| Lighting | 500–700 lux, D65 (daylight 6500K) | Consistent color evaluation |
| Background | Neutral gray (N5–N7, Munsell) | Prevents contrast bias |
| Airflow | Low, filtered, no drafts | Prevents odor cross-contamination |
| Sound level | < 45 dB | Minimize distraction |
| Sample preparation bench | Separate from evaluation area | Prevents olfactory fatigue |

---

## 3. Panel Qualification

Sensory evaluation should be conducted by a **trained panel** of minimum 3 assessors (preferably 5–8). Assessors must:

- Pass basic taste recognition screening (sweet, sour, salty, bitter, umami)
- Pass odor recognition test (≥ 8 of 12 common spice odors)
- Not consume food/drink (except water) for 30 minutes prior to evaluation
- Not use scented personal products on evaluation days

---

## 4. Evaluation Protocol

### 4.1 Visual Assessment

Score each criterion on a **1–5 scale** (1 = unacceptable, 3 = acceptable, 5 = excellent).

| Criterion | How to Assess | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 |
|:---------:|:-------------:|:-:|:-:|:-:|:-:|:-:|
| **Color intensity** | Visual comparison against reference standard | Pale/dull | Slightly dull | Moderate | Bright | Very intense |
| **Color uniformity** | Visual scan across sample; note streaking | Unacceptable variation | Noticeable variation | Slight variation | Uniform | Perfectly uniform |
| **Particle consistency** | Visual check for fines/lumps/excessive dust | Very uneven | Moderately uneven | Slightly uneven | Mostly even | Perfectly even |
| **Foreign matter** | Visible stones, stems, dark specks | > 5 specks | 3–5 specks | 1–2 specks | None visible | None |
| **Gloss/texture** | Surface sheen under light | Dull, dusty | Slightly dull | Neutral | Slight sheen | Natural oily sheen |

### 4.2 Aroma Assessment

| Criterion | Descriptors | How to Assess | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 |
|:---------:|:-----------:|:-------------:|:-:|:-:|:-:|:-:|:-:|
| **Sweet bell pepper** | Typical red bell pepper note | Sniff 5 cm from open dish | None | Faint | Moderate | Pronounced | Intense |
| **Smoky/earthy** | Drying process note | Same method | Overpowering | Noticeable | Slight | Trace | None |
| **Freshness** | No musty/stale note | Same method | Musty/stale | Slightly stale | Neutral | Fresh | Very fresh |
| **Pungency/heat** | Capsaicin note | Inhale gently (do not deeply inhale powder) | Too intense | Noticeable | Mild | Slight | None |

### 4.3 Flavor Assessment

**Method:** Prepare a 0.5% suspension (1 g paprika in 200 mL warm water, 50°C). Stir, let stand 2 min, then taste 10 mL.

| Criterion | Descriptor | Score Range |
|:---------:|:----------:|:-----------:|
| **Sweetness** | Characteristic red pepper sweetness | 1–5 |
| **Bitterness** | Undesirable in quality paprika; note seed/green material | 1 (excessive) – 5 (none) |
| **Astringency** | Drying sensation on palate | 1 (excessive) – 5 (none) |
| **Mouthfeel** | Powder feel — smooth vs. gritty | 1 (gritty) – 5 (silky) |
| **Aftertaste** | Residual sensation 30 sec after swallowing | 1 (unpleasant lingering) – 5 (clean) |

### 4.4 Tactile Assessment (for whole pods only)

| Criterion | Good | Poor |
|:---------:|:----:|:----:|
| **Pod flexibility** | Brittle enough to crack (≤ 12% moisture) | Leathery or elastic (> 12% moisture) |
| **Seed attachment** | Seeds separate cleanly | Seeds embedded in flesh (immature harvest) |
| **Flesh thickness** | 0.8–1.5 mm (depends on variety) | < 0.6 mm (thin, stemmy) |

## 5. Scoring & Release Criteria

| Score Range | Classification | Action |
|:-----------:|:--------------:|:-------|
| 4.0–5.0 | Excellent | Release for premium market |
| 3.0–3.9 | Acceptable | Release for standard market |
| 2.0–2.9 | Marginal | Hold; re-evaluate with instrumental tests |
| 1.0–1.9 | Reject | Do not ship; enter CAPA |

**Minimum Passing Score by Product Category:**

| Category | Min Overall Score | Min Color Score | Min Aroma Score |
|:--------:|:-----------------:|:---------------:|:---------------:|
| Premium (ASTA 160+) | 4.0 | 4.0 | 4.0 |
| Superior (ASTA 120+) | 3.5 | 3.5 | 3.5 |
| Standard (ASTA 80+) | 3.0 | 3.0 | 3.0 |
| Flakes (all grades) | 3.0 | 3.0 | 3.0 |
| Whole pods | 3.0 | 3.0 | 3.0 |
