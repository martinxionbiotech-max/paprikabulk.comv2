# Specifications — Product Specifications

Complete technical specifications for all paprika product forms and grades. Each spec sheet provides comprehensive data on chemical, physical, microbiological, and regulatory parameters.

## How to Read These Specifications

Each product spec sheet includes the following standardized sections:

- **Overview** — Product description, grade, and typical applications with customer segment
- **Scientific Classification** — Botanical name (*Capsicum annuum* L.), origin, harvest season
- **Chemical Specifications** — ASTA color value, moisture, total ash, acid-insoluble ash, volatile oils
- **Physical Specifications** — Particle size distribution (mesh analysis), bulk density (loose & tapped), color (CIELAB L*a*b*)
- **Microbiological Limits** — APC, Yeast & Mold, Salmonella, E. coli, Bacillus cereus
- **Heavy Metals** — Pb, Cd, As, Hg limits with regulatory source references
- **Mycotoxin Limits** — Aflatoxin B1, total aflatoxins, ochratoxin A
- **Storage & Shelf Life** — Temperature, humidity, packaging requirements, expected shelf life
- **Certifications** — HACCP, ISO, Kosher, Organic, Gluten-Free (as applicable per product)
- **Applications** — Specific use cases with recommended usage rates

## Grade Classification Summary

Paprika grades are primarily classified by ASTA color value, the international standard for color measurement:

| Grade | ASTA Color Value | Color Description | Price Index (vs Superior) | Typical Application |
|:------|:----------------:|:-----------------:|:-------------------------:|:--------------------|
| Special | 200+ | Deepest red, maximum color intensity | 1.5–2.0× | Oleoresin extraction, premium extracts |
| Premium | 160–200 | Deep red, high color yield | 1.2–1.5× | Processed meats, surimi, gourmet foods |
| Superior | 120–160 | Medium red, good color value | 1.0× (baseline) | Premium seasoning blends, snack seasonings |
| Standard | 80–120 | Moderate red, cost-effective | 0.7–0.85× | General food coloring, soups, sauces |

## Application Guide

| Application | Recommended Grade | Form | Why |
|:------------|:-----------------|:----:|:----|
| Processed meats (sausages, surimi) | Premium (160–200) | Powder | Deep red color retention through cooking (a*/b* ≥ 1.8) |
| Premium seasoning blends | Superior (120–160) | Powder | Good color at economical cost |
| Dry rubs & marinades | Premium (160–200) | Flakes | Visual impact on meat surface |
| Sauces & soups | Standard (80–120) | Powder | Cost-effective for liquid applications |
| Snack seasonings | Superior (120–160) | Powder | Good adhesion, even color distribution |
| Oleoresin extraction | Special (200+) | Powder | Maximum color yield per kg raw material |
| Gourmet garnish | Premium (160–200) | Flakes | Visual appeal on finished dishes |
| General food coloring | Standard (80–120) | Powder | Lowest cost for acceptable color level |
| R&D sample requests | Any grade | As specified | Product development and evaluation |

## Specification Compliance Checklist

When reviewing a product specification from a supplier, use this checklist to ensure completeness:

| Check | Item | Why It Matters |
|:----:|:-----|:---------------|
| ☐ | ASTA color value range specified with method reference | Basis for acceptance/rejection |
| ☐ | Moisture maximum limit | Affects trade weight and shelf life |
| ☐ | Particle size distribution (mesh analysis) | Determines application suitability |
| ☐ | Total ash and acid-insoluble ash limits | Quality and cleanliness indicators |
| ☐ | Microbiological limits with method references | Food safety compliance |
| ☐ | Heavy metals limits per destination market | Regulatory compliance |
| ☐ | Mycotoxin limits per destination market | EU requirement (mandatory) |
| ☐ | Pesticide MRL compliance statement | Regulatory compliance |
| ☐ | Shelf life statement | Inventory management |
| ☐ | Test methods explicitly referenced | Dispute resolution basis |
