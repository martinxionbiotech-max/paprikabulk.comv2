**Description:** Standard Grade paprika powder offers a cost-effective solution for applications where moderate red color (ASTA 80–120) is acceptable and heat level tolerance is higher (≤2,000 SHU). Produced from mixed-origin *Capsicum annuum* L. harvests, this grade delivers consistent quality for large-volume buyers who prioritize unit economics over maximum color intensity. Available in kraft paper or PP woven bag packaging.

## Application Notes

Standard Grade paprika powder (ASTA 80–120) is the **volume workhorse** for markets where paprika is used primarily for identity rather than intense coloring.

| Application Segment | Suitability | Notes |
|---------------------|-------------|-------|
| Domestic Chinese food processing | ★★★★★ | Widely used in spice blends for local market |
| Industrial seasoning (volume) | ★★★★☆ | Good value for large-batch manufacturing |
| Meat processing (cost-sensitive) | ★★★★☆ | Acceptable for basic products |
| Snack seasonings (value tier) | ★★★☆☆ | Works with higher loading rates to compensate for color |
| Sauces & condiments (basic) | ★★★★☆ | Use at 1–2% for moderate color |

## Processing Recommendations

### Blending & Optimization
- **Color boosting:** Blend Standard Grade (70%) with Premium (20%) and oleoresin (10%) to achieve ASTA 160 at lower cost than 100% Premium.
- **Heat masking:** If the 2,000 SHU upper limit is too high for a mild application, request heat-reduced lots (blending with sweet paprika varieties can lower SHU to ≤800).
- **Moisture management:** Due to the wider origin mix, Standard Grade lots may have slightly higher moisture variability — confirm moisture on each batch COA.

### Dosing Recommendations
- **Industrial seasonings:** 5–15% w/w of blend
- **Meat processing:** 0.3–1.0% w/w
- **Soups & sauces:** 0.5–2.0% w/w

### Processing Caution
Because Standard Grade has lower ASTA than Premium/Superior, compensate by increasing loading rate or blending with oleoresin. DO NOT compensate by increasing heat exposure — paprika color degrades at similar rates regardless of starting ASTA.

## Storage Stability Data

| Condition | Shelf Life | Expected Outcome |
|-----------|------------|------------------|
| 15–25°C, ≤60% RH, dark | 12 months | ASTA loss 10–15% |
| 25–30°C, ≤65% RH, dark | 9 months | ASTA loss 15–25% |
| >30°C or >65% RH | 6 months max | Significant degradation risk |
| Sealed, unopened | 12 months | Per condition above |
| After opening | 2–4 months | Reseal immediately |

**Critical:**
- Store away from direct sunlight.
- Maintain RH ≤60% to prevent mold growth.
- Do not freeze or refrigerate.
