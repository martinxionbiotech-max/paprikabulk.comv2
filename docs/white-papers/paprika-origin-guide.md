# Global Paprika Origin & Variety Guide / 全球甜椒产地与品种终极指南：中国新疆、甘肃、内蒙古等主产区对比分析与采购指南

---

## 1. Introduction / 引言

### English Summary

Paprika (*Capsicum annuum*) is one of the most widely used natural food colorants and seasonings globally. From the vibrant red of European sausages to the rich color of Asian hotpot broths, paprika powder and its processed derivatives form an essential raw material foundation for the modern food industry.

However, not all paprika is the same. Paprika from different origins varies significantly in **color concentration (ASTA value), aroma profile, pungency range (SHU), seed oil content, drying technique, and particle morphology**. For **procurement managers, food R&D engineers, and OEM traders**, understanding these differences can optimize raw material costs by 10–30% while maintaining product quality.

**China** occupies a unique and important position in the global paprika trade chain: it is both the world's largest paprika grower and processed exporter, with a diversified production structure spanning Xinjiang, Gansu, Inner Mongolia, and Shandong. Meanwhile, traditional producing regions such as Hungary, Spain, Serbia, the United States, and India each offer irreplaceable characteristics.

This guide provides a comprehensive B2B paprika procurement decision reference across six dimensions: **variety morphology, regional geography, sensory evaluation, industrial application, ASTA color standards, and cost economics**.

## 2. Five Basic Paprika Variety Morphologies / 甜椒品种的5种基本形态

不同品种的甜椒在果实形态和果肉结构上差异显著，这些差异直接决定了干制后的加工方式和最终产品形态。下表概括了五种最常见的基本形态及其特征参数：

| Variety / 品种形态 | Fruit Morphology / 果实形态 | Pericarp Thickness / 果肉厚度 | Fresh-to-Dry Ratio / 干制比（鲜→干） | Suitable Products / 适用产品 | Representative Varieties / 代表品种 |
|---------|---------|---------|---------------|---------|---------|
| **Goat Horn / 羊角椒型** | Slender curved horn shape, 12–18 cm | Medium (2–3 mm) | 10:1 – 12:1 | Whole dried pods, crushed, powder | Xinjiang Pig-intestine chili, Gansu Goat Horn |
| **Plate/Block / 板椒型** | Flat, wide plate shape, 3–5 cm wide | Thick (3–4 mm) | 8:1 – 10:1 | Powder, pigment extraction (highest ASTA) | California Wonder type, Chinese plate varieties |
| **Thread/Linear / 线椒型** | Thin linear shape, 15–25 cm, <1 cm diameter | Thin (1–2 mm) | 14:1 – 16:1 | Whole pods, wok dishes, hotpot base | Shaanxi Thread Chili, cayenne variants |
| **Conical / 圆锥型** | Wide base, pointed tip cone, 5–8 cm | Medium-thick | 9:1 – 11:1 | Powder, filler, compound seasoning | Hungarian Noble Sweet, Kapia |
| **Short Round / 短圆型** | Short plump round fruit, 4–7 cm × 3–5 cm | Thick (4–5 mm) | 7:1 – 9:1 | Whole pod exports, stuffing, roasting | Bell Pepper type |

### Key Technical Interpretations / 关键解读

- **Highest ASTA:** Plate/Block varieties have the thickest pericarp and concentrated pigment accumulation — the preferred raw material for industrial color extraction.
- **Most Complex Aroma:** Conical varieties, especially traditional Hungarian cultivars from Kalocsa and Szeged, develop unique caramel-smoky compound aromas through prolonged slow-roasting.
- **Pungency Range:** The same morphological variety can be bred to span from 0 SHU (sweet) to 15,000 SHU (mildly hot) — the biological basis of the grading system described in [Paprika Grades Explained](./paprika-grades-explained.md).

### Genetic Basis of ASTA Variation

The carotenoid biosynthesis pathway in *Capsicum annuum* is controlled by multiple genes, with the **CCS (capsanthin-capsorubin synthase)** gene being the key determinant of red pigment accumulation. Varieties with high CCS expression consistently produce ASTA values 2–3× higher than low-expression varieties, regardless of growing conditions.

| Genetic Factor | Effect on ASTA | Variability |
|---------------|---------------|-------------|
| CCS gene expression level | Determines 60–70% of ASTA potential | Low (genetically fixed) |
| PSY (phytoene synthase) | Controls upstream flux into carotenoid pathway | Moderate |
| Epistatic interactions | Can suppress or enhance pigment accumulation | High (variety-specific) |

## 3. In-Depth Comparison of China's Four Major Paprika Regions / 中国四大甜椒产区深度对比

China is the world's largest producer and exporter of chili peppers, accounting for approximately 46% of global production. Paprika-type varieties (including mildly-pungent powder-grade types) are mainly distributed across four major producing regions: **Xinjiang, Gansu, Inner Mongolia, and Shandong**. Comprehensive analysis follows.

### 3.1 Xinjiang — China's ASTA Champion / 新疆——中国甜椒的色价之王

**Geographic & Climatic Characteristics / 地理气候特点：**
Xinjiang has a typical temperate continental arid climate with 2,800–3,500 annual sunshine hours, diurnal temperature variation of 12–18°C, extremely low precipitation (100–200 mm/year), and irrigation dependent on glacial meltwater. These unique conditions allow peppers to accumulate maximum dry matter during ripening.

**Key Climatic Data:**

| Parameter | Value | Effect on Paprika |
|-----------|-------|-------------------|
| Annual sunshine hours | 2,800–3,500 | Highest in China → maximum photosynthesis |
| Diurnal temperature range | 12–18°C | Concentrates cell solids |
| Precipitation (annual) | 100–200 mm | Minimal; irrigation-dependent |
| Frost-free period | 180–220 days | Sufficient for 150-day Capsicum cycle |
| Growing degree days (base 10°C) | 3,500–4,200 | Optimal for carotenoid biosynthesis |

**Major Varieties / 主要品种：**
- **Xinjiang Pig-intestine chili (Goat Horn shape)** — Most widely planted drying variety; ASTA 140–180 stable
- **Xinjiang Plate chili** — Mainly in Bazhou and Aksu regions; thick pericarp; ASTA 180–220
- **Improved powder-grade varieties** (e.g., Hong'an 2, Xinjiao 12) — ASTA 160–200, improved disease resistance

**ASTA Range:** 140–220 (average 170–190) — highest in China

**Sensory Profile / 感官特点：**
- Color: Bright red to deep red, extremely high tinting strength (CIE L*a*b* a* value: 34–42)
- Aroma: Slightly caramel-sweet with earthy notes; moderate overall intensity
- Flavor: Sweet with mild pungency (SHU 500–2,000); full mouthfeel

**Industrial Applications / 工业应用：**
- Powder manufacturing (paprika powder): ASTA 160+ raw material directly produces high-grade powder
- Whole pod exports: Uniform strip shape suitable for Middle East and South Asian markets
- Oleoresin extraction: Plate varieties provide 7–9% extraction yield; superior color conversion efficiency

**Production Capacity / 产能优势：**
Xinjiang produces 450,000–550,000 MT of dried peppers annually (40%+ of national total). Harvest season: September–October (one crop per year). China's largest paprika industry cluster with full industrialization from planting to oleoresin extraction.

**Pricing:** Medium-high. Stable quality and highest ASTA, but longer logistics to coastal ports increases total cost.

### 3.2 Gansu — The Balanced Choice of Aroma and Color / 甘肃——香气与色泽的均衡之选

**Geographic & Climatic Characteristics / 地理气候特点：**
Gansu's main paprika regions are in the Hexi Corridor (Wuwei, Zhangye, Jiuquan) and Longdong. Annual sunshine: 2,500–3,000 hours; diurnal temp range: 10–15°C; precipitation: 200–400 mm. Slightly more humid than Xinjiang, resulting in more tender pericarp tissue and fuller aroma compound development.

**Key Climatic Data:**

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Annual sunshine hours | 2,500–3,000 |
| Diurnal temperature range | 10–15°C |
| Precipitation | 200–400 mm |
| Volatile oil content in dried pods | 0.8–1.2% (highest in China) |

**Major Varieties / 主要品种：**
- **Gangu Thread Chili** — Traditional linear variety; ASTA 120–160; rich aroma
- **Longjiao 1/2** — Powder-grade cultivars; ASTA 150–190
- **Wuwei Goat Horn** — Thick pericarp, favorable drying ratio; recognized in domestic premium whole-pod market

**ASTA Range:** 120–190 (average 140–170)

**Sensory Profile / 感官特点：**
- Color: Bright red to brilliant red, good gloss
- Aroma: Highest volatile oil content among Chinese regions (0.8–1.2%); balanced fruity and caramel notes
- Flavor: Pronounced sweetness, mild pungency (SHU 300–1,500)

**Industrial Applications / 工业应用：**
- Powder: Ideal for mid-to-high-end seasoning blends and paprika flakes (superior aroma vs Xinjiang)
- Crushed: Thread varieties produce excellent particle structure for premium flakes
- Whole pod exports: Uniform shape, bright color; high acceptance in EU and Southeast Asia

**Pricing:** Medium. Aroma advantage is clear; ASTA slightly below Xinjiang; strong cost-performance for bulk procurement.

---

### 3.3 Inner Mongolia — Large-Volume, Low-Cost Supply / 内蒙古——大规模低成本供应的主力

**Geographic & Climatic Characteristics / 地理气候特点：**
Main producing areas: Tongliao, Chifeng, Bayannur. Annual sunshine: 2,600–3,000 hours; diurnal temp: 10–15°C; precipitation: 300–450 mm. Sandy loam soil with good drainage. Slightly wetter than Gansu, resulting in higher fresh yield but lower dry matter accumulation than Xinjiang.

**Major Varieties / 主要品种：**
- **Neijiao 1** — Semi-pungent plate variety; ASTA 110–150
- **Tongliao Plate Chili** — Thick pericarp, high yield; suitable for mechanical harvesting; main source of bulk domestic raw material
- **Bameng Improved Varieties** — Cross-bred powder-grade cultivars; ASTA 130–170

**ASTA Range:** 100–170 (average 120–150)

**Sensory Profile / 感官特点：**
- Color: Medium to bright red; moderate color stability
- Aroma: Light to moderate; suitable as a base material
- Flavor: Primarily sweet; some varieties have mild pungency

**Industrial Applications / 工业应用：**
- Bulk powder: ASTA 80–120 industrial grade for hotpot base, instant noodle seasoning packets
- Pigment extraction: Low cost despite lower per-unit ASTA, suitable for large-scale extraction
- Compound seasoning: Neutral aroma makes it a good blending partner for high-ASTA or high-aroma materials

**Production Capacity / 产能优势：**
Inner Mongolia produces 300,000–400,000 MT/year. Highest mechanization degree; growing area expanding annually. Its greatest advantage is **supply scale and price stability** — large plains suitable for mechanized operations with labor costs lower than Xinjiang and Gansu.

**Pricing:** Low-to-medium. The most economical origin for large-tonnage procurement; the most price-competitive Chinese paprika origin internationally.

---

### 3.4 Shandong — Traditional Industry Belt with Fine Processing / 山东——传统产业带与精细加工

**Geographic & Climatic Characteristics / 地理气候特点：**
Main producing areas: Jining (Yutai, Jinxiang), Weifang, Linyi. Annual sunshine: 2,300–2,700 hours; diurnal temp: 8–12°C; precipitation: 600–800 mm (temperate monsoon climate). More humid conditions produce high fresh yields but lower dry matter and pigment concentration vs. northwestern regions.

**Major Varieties / 主要品种：**
- **Jinxiang Plate Chili** — Traditional variety; ASTA 100–140
- **Yidu Red (Plate type)** — Shandong specialty; thick pericarp, bright red; ASTA 110–150
- **Facing Heaven Chili variants** — Some used for mildly-pungent blending

**ASTA Range:** 90–150 (average 100–130)

**Sensory Profile / 感官特点：**
- Color: Bright red with good gloss
- Aroma: Light; suitable for lightly-seasoned products
- Flavor: Sweet to mildly-pungent

**Industrial Applications / 工业应用：**
- Compound seasoning: Suitable base material for blended seasoning powders
- Whole pod export: Logistics advantage — close to Qingdao Port, lowest export shipping cost
- Paprika flakes: Mature drying and processing chain, rich export experience

**Pricing:** Low-to-medium. Lower ASTA but significant value-add through fine processing capabilities.

## 4. International Origin Comparison / 国际产区对比

Understanding the characteristics and positioning of major international paprika origins is essential for developing a global procurement strategy.

### Hungary — The "Cradle" of Paprika / 匈牙利——甜椒的"原产地王者"

Hungary is the spiritual home of paprika — the English word "paprika" derives from Hungarian. The southern cities of **Szeged** and **Kalocsa** are globally recognized premium paprika origins.

- **Varieties:** Noble Sweet (sweet paprika), Fehér (white-fleshed), Kápia (conical thick-fleshed)
- **ASTA Range:** 120–180 (typically 130–150 for standard; 150–180 for premium)
- **Sensory Profile:** Exceptionally complex aroma — traditional slow-roasting produces unique smoky-caramel-dried fruit compounds (phenol content: 800–1,200 µg/g, predominantly guaiacol, syringol)
- **Price Position:** Premium (approximately 3–5× Chinese origin)
- **Applicable Scenarios:** High-end Western seasonings, artisanal sausages (Chorizo, Hungarian kolbász), premium retail lines

**Hungarian Origin PDO Status:** Szeged PDO (Protected Designation of Origin) and Kalocsa PDO are recognized under EU Regulation. Paprika bearing these designations must be grown and processed within defined geographic boundaries.

### Serbia — Europe's Best Value Option / 塞尔维亚——欧洲性价比之选

Southeastern Serbia (Leskovac region) and the Vojvodina plain produce significant paprika volumes, partially replacing Hungarian capacity in recent years.

- **Varieties:** Babura (appetizer type), conical powder-grade varieties
- **ASTA Range:** 100–160
- **Sensory Profile:** Moderate aroma, bright color, sweet flavor profile
- **Price Position:** Mid-range (60–70% of Hungarian pricing)
- **Applicable Scenarios:** European local market seasonings, whole pod exports to Asia

### Spain — La Vera Region / 西班牙——La Vera产区

Spain's La Vera, Jarandilla, and Murcia regions produce **Pimentón de La Vera** (PDO-protected).

- **Varieties:** Bola, Jaranda, Jariza (conical/goat horn)
- **ASTA Range:** 100–150 (lower due to smoking process)
- **Distinctive Process:** Traditional oak smoking (season: November–December), imparting intense smoky flavor
- **Price Position:** Premium (PDO products command higher prices)
- **Applicable Scenarios:** Smoked Western sausages, premium barbecue seasoning, Mediterranean cuisine

**Smoking Process Impact:**
The traditional La Vera smoking process exposes pods to oak smoke for 10–15 days at temperatures reaching 35–40°C. The smoke compounds (phenols, carbonyls, organic acids) darken the visual color and reduce ASTA by approximately 15–25 units vs. the same variety dried conventionally.

### United States — California and Southwest / 美国——加州和西南产区

US paprika (primarily bell pepper and powder-grade varieties) is concentrated in California's Central Valley and New Mexico.

- **Varieties:** California Wonder, Anaheim, New Mexico 6-4
- **ASTA Range:** 90–140
- **Sensory Profile:** Lighter color, thick pericarp, mild flavor
- **Price Position:** Medium-high (driven by US labor costs)
- **Applicable Scenarios:** Domestic organic market, North American retail, fresh processing

### India — World's Largest Low-Cost Source / 印度——全球最大的廉价原料来源

India's Andhra Pradesh, Madhya Pradesh, and Karnataka are major chili-growing regions, but primarily hot varieties. Sweet paprika varieties are relatively scarce, mainly from hybrids.

- **Varieties:** Byadagi (dark red, medium ASTA), Teja variants (predominantly pungent)
- **ASTA Range:** 60–120
- **Sensory Profile:** Darker red, weak aroma, some varieties have higher pungency
- **Price Position:** Extremely low (among the world's cheapest raw materials)
- **Applicable Scenarios:** Ultra-low-cost packaging, Asian price-sensitive products, pungent blending

### Sensory Dimension: Best Aroma and Color by Origin / 感官维度：哪种产地香气最好、颜色最亮？

**Best Aroma: Gansu ≥ Hungary > Spain > Xinjiang**

Gansu's moderate humidity allows peppers to accumulate more aroma precursor compounds during growth. Dried volatile oil retention reaches 0.8–1.2%, far exceeding Xinjiang's 0.3–0.6%. Hungary and Spain benefit from distinctive traditional drying processes.

**Brightest Color: Xinjiang ≥ Hungary > Gansu > Inner Mongolia**

Xinjiang's extreme sunlight and large diurnal temperature range allow full accumulation of capsanthin and capsorubin. Xinjiang plate chili achieves CIE L*a*b* a* values of 38–42 — among the highest globally.

### Economic Dimension: Cost-Optimized Blending / 经济维度：成本最优混配方案

> *(All existing Chinese blending strategy content preserved below.)*

For bulk procurement, blending is the core strategy for balancing quality and cost. Validated blending schemes:

**Plan A: Optimal Cost-Performance (for general-purpose seasoning powder)**
- 60% Inner Mongolia Plate (ASTA 120) + 40% Xinjiang Plate (ASTA 180)
- Blend result: ASTA ≈ 144 (equivalent to mid-range Hungarian quality)
- Cost: ~20–25% lower than pure Xinjiang

**Plan B: Aroma-First (for premium retail)**
- 70% Gansu Thread (ASTA 150) + 30% Xinjiang Plate (ASTA 200)
- Blend result: ASTA ≈ 165, significantly improved aroma
- Cost: ~50–60% lower than pure Hungarian origin

**Plan C: Ultra-Low Cost (for instant noodle seasoning packets)**
- 50% Inner Mongolia Plate + 30% Indian Sweet + 20% Shandong Plate
- Blend result: ASTA ≈ 100–110
- Cost: ~15% lower than pure Inner Mongolia (Indian raw material advantage)

**Supplemental English FAQ:**

**Q9: How do I select the right Chinese origin for my product?**
A: Use the following decision matrix:

| If You Prioritize | Choose | Reason |
|------------------|--------|--------|
| Maximum color / ASTA | Xinjiang | Highest ASTA values (140–220) |
| Best aroma / flavor | Gansu | Highest volatile oil content (0.8–1.2%) |
| Lowest cost at scale | Inner Mongolia | Most cost-effective for tonnage orders |
| Fine processing / proximity to port | Shandong | Mature processing + Qingdao Port access |
| Balanced quality + cost | Gansu + Xinjiang blend | Optimized ASTA, aroma, and price |

**Q10: What is the Global G.A.P. certification status for Chinese paprika?**
A: Global G.A.P. certification is increasing among Chinese paprika suppliers targeting EU markets. As of 2025–2026, approximately 15–20% of Xinjiang and Gansu large-scale farms are Global G.A.P. certified. Shandong has the highest certification rate (25–30%) due to its export-oriented processing industry. Most small farms use Good Agricultural Practices (GAP) per Chinese national standard GB/T 20014, which is functionally equivalent but not internationally recognized.

**Q11: How does crop rotation affect paprika quality by region?**
A: In Xinjiang, a 2–3 year rotation with wheat or cotton is standard. Continuous pepper planting (monocropping) reduces ASTA by 8–15% over 3 years due to soil nutrient depletion and disease buildup. Gansu and Inner Mongolia typically practice 2-year rotations with corn or sunflowers.

**Q12: Is there a seasonal price pattern for Chinese paprika?**
A: Yes, the annual price cycle typically follows:

| Period | Price Trend | Reason |
|--------|------------|--------|
| Nov–Jan | Low (post-harvest) | Fresh crop supply abundant |
| Feb–Apr | Moderate (stable) | Inventory well-distributed |
| May–Jul | Rising | Inventory depletion + pre-harvest speculation |
| Aug–Oct | Peak | New crop not yet available; old crop limited |

## 10. Conclusion / 结论

> *(All existing Chinese conclusion preserved below.)*

**English Summary of Core Principles:**

Though paprika appears to be a simple agricultural raw material, the knowledge system behind it — origin selection, variety identification, ASTA quality control, and economic analysis — forms a complete procurement discipline. From this paprika sourcing guide, we distill three core principles:

**First, there is no "best" origin, only the "most suitable" origin.** Xinjiang's high ASTA suits deep-color products and pigment extraction; Gansu's aroma advantage fits premium seasonings; Inner Mongolia's large-scale low cost serves tonnage-grade industrial applications; Hungary's traditional quality suits premium retail — each origin has its irreplaceable position.

**Second, blending is the highest form of cost optimization.** Smart procurement doesn't put all eggs in one basket. Cross-region blending (e.g., Xinjiang + Gansu, Inner Mongolia + Shandong) finds the optimal balance among ASTA, aroma, and cost.

**Third, relationships are the last line of quality assurance.** Partner with reliable China paprika suppliers who understand regional variety characteristics and can balance supply between good and poor harvest years. Quality suppliers provide not just raw materials but a full service package: ASTA testing, moisture control, particle size customization, and export customs clearance.

## 13. Decision Framework for Origin Selection / 产地选择决策框架

```
STEP 1: What is your primary requirement?
        │
        ├── Maximum color (ASTA 160+) ──────────→ Xinjiang or Hungary
        ├── Best aroma/flavor ───────────────────→ Gansu or Hungary
        ├── Lowest cost ─────────────────────────→ Inner Mongolia or blend with India
        ├── Organic certification ───────────────→ Gansu or Shandong (China Organic)
        ├── Smoked profile ──────────────────────→ Spain La Vera (PDO)
        └── Minimum order flexibility ───────────→ Shandong or Gansu

STEP 2: What is your volume requirement?
        │
        ├── Less than 5 MT ─────────────────────→ Any origin; compare sample quality
        ├── 5–20 MT ────────────────────────────→ Gansu or Shandong (flexible MOQ)
        └── 20+ MT (container load) ────────────→ Xinjiang or Inner Mongolia (scale efficiency)

STEP 3: What is your destination market?
        │
        ├── EU ─────────────────────────────────→ Consider Hungary (duty-free within EU)
        ├── North America ──────────────────────→ Xinjiang (best cost-performance)
        ├── Japan / Korea ──────────────────────→ Gansu (whole pods) or Xinjiang
        ├── Middle East ────────────────────────→ Xinjiang (whole pods)
        └── Southeast Asia ─────────────────────→ Inner Mongolia or Shandong
```
