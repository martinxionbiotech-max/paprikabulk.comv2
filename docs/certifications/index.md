This section documents the food safety and quality certifications maintained by Dinweys (Qingdao).Co.,Ltd for all paprika products. Certifications are verified by accredited third-party certification bodies and updated annually.

## Why Certifications Matter in B2B Paprika Trade

Certifications serve as the foundation of trust in international spice trade. For B2B buyers, verified certifications provide:

- **Regulatory compliance assurance** — Meet destination market requirements (EU, US, Japan, others)
- **Risk reduction** — Third-party verification reduces the need for buyer-conducted audits
- **Supply chain transparency** — Clear documentation of quality management systems
- **Market access** — Many retailers and food manufacturers require specific certifications from suppliers
- **Brand protection** — Certified suppliers reduce the risk of recalls and reputation damage

### Due Diligence: How Buyers Use Certifications

| Buyer Type | Typical Certification Requirement | Verification Method |
|:-----------|:---------------------------------|:--------------------|
| Food manufacturers | HACCP + ISO 22000 | Review certificate + annual audit report |
| Retailers | HACCP + GFSI benchmarked scheme | Certification body database check |
| Importers (US) | HACCP + FSMA compliance | Certificate + FSVP documentation |
| Importers (EU) | HACCP + ESA compliance | Certificate + EU importer verification |
| Organic buyers | USDA or EU Organic | Certifier database + batch traceability |
| Kosher-sensitive buyers | Kosher certification | Certifying agency's public registry |

## Individual Certification Pages

### Food Safety Management

- [HACCP](haccp.md) — Hazard Analysis Critical Control Point plan covering all production stages from raw material receiving to finished product dispatch. Includes critical limit documentation, CCP monitoring records, and corrective action procedures. HACCP is a prerequisite for doing business with most food manufacturers and is required under FSMA Preventive Controls rule.

- [ISO](iso.md) — Integrated management system certifications: ISO 9001 (Quality Management), ISO 22000 (Food Safety Management), and ISO 14001 (Environmental Management). All maintained with annual surveillance audits by accredited certification bodies. These certifications demonstrate a systematic approach to quality, food safety, and environmental responsibility.

### Dietary & Religious Certifications

- [Kosher](kosher.md) — Kashrut certification ensuring all paprika products meet Jewish dietary law requirements. Certified by a recognized Kosher agency with annual renewal. Covers all production lines and storage facilities. For paprika, Kosher certification is straightforward as the product itself is inherently kosher, but the certification verifies no cross-contamination during processing.

- [Organic](organic.md) — Dual certification for USDA National Organic Program (NOP) and EU Organic Regulation. Traceable from farm to finished product with full batch-level documentation of organic integrity. Organic paprika commands a significant price premium and requires segregated processing lines and storage.

- [Gluten-Free](gluten-free.md) — Third-party tested gluten-free certification with documented limits below 20 ppm. Includes cross-contamination prevention procedures in shared facilities. While paprika is naturally gluten-free, certification provides assurance to buyers with celiac-safety requirements.

## How to Choose the Right Certifications

### For US Market Importers

- FDA FSMA compliance documentation including FSVP plan
- HACCP plan documentation per 21 CFR Part 117
- Organic certification (USDA NOP) if organic product
- Gluten-Free certification if catering to allergen-free market

### For EU Market Importers

- HACCP / Codex Alimentarius compliance as minimum
- ISO 22000 preferred for premium buyers
- EU Organic certification (Regulation EU 2018/848) if organic
- EU Pesticide MRL compliance documentation

### For Processed Food Manufacturers

- ISO 9001 + ISO 22000 (dual management system)
- Kosher certification (if targeting Kosher market segments)
- Gluten-Free certification (allergen management program)
- Supplier audit reports as part of own supplier approval program
