## Category 1: Spice Science

Technical definitions for chemical, physical, and sensory properties of paprika.

| Term | Definition | Key Standard |
|:-----|:-----------|:-------------|
| [ASTA](spice-science/asta.md) | American Spice Trade Association color value — the primary color quality metric for paprika. Measured as absorbance of acetone extract at 460 nm. Range: 80–260+ for commercial grades. Calculation: ASTA = (Absorbance × 164 × DF) / (Sample weight in g). | ASTA Method 20.1, ISO 7541 |
| [Capsaicin](spice-science/capsaicin.md) | The pungent compound responsible for heat in chili peppers. Measured in Scoville Heat Units (SHU) or HPLC-based ASTA pungency units. Sweet paprika contains negligible levels (< 0.01%). Structure: 8-methyl-N-vanillyl-6-nonenamide. | HPLC per ASTA 21, ISO 3513 |
| [Capsanthin](spice-science/capsanthin.md) | The primary red carotenoid pigment in paprika, constituting 30–60% of total carotenoids. Molecular weight: 584.9 g/mol. Responsible for the characteristic deep red color and the primary contributor to E160c food colorant. | HPLC quantification per CIE |
| [Capsicum](spice-science/capsicum.md) | The botanical genus encompassing all chili pepper and paprika varieties. Five domesticated species: *C. annuum* (most common for paprika), *C. frutescens*, *C. chinense*, *C. baccatum*, *C. pubescens*. | Botanical classification |
| [Carotenoids](spice-science/carotenoids.md) | A family of red, orange, and yellow pigments naturally present in paprika. Includes capsanthin, capsorubin, β-carotene, zeaxanthin, and lutein. Total carotenoid content ranges 200–600 mg/100g. Red-to-yellow ratio determines visual hue. | HPLC per ISO |
| [Moisture](spice-science/moisture.md) | Water content of paprika, critically affecting microbial stability, color degradation rate, and flowability. Maximum limits: 11% (ESA), 12% (ASTA). Measured by oven drying at 103°C ± 2°C to constant weight. | ISO 939, AOAC 925.10 |
| [Oleoresin](spice-science/oleoresin.md) | Concentrated extract of paprika using solvent (typically hexane or ethanol) extraction. Contains both color pigments (20–60 g/kg) and flavor compounds. Used as natural food colorant E160c in processed foods. Multiple extraction grades available. | Solvent extraction per EFSA |
| [Particle Size](spice-science/particle-size.md) | Granulometric distribution measured by sieve analysis using ASTM E11 sieves. Determines dissolution rate, color release, and application suitability. Expressed as mesh size or percentage retained/passing specified screens. | ASTM E11, ISO 3310 |
| [SHU / Scoville](spice-science/shu.md) | Scoville Heat Units — the historical measure of pungency developed by Wilbur Scoville in 1912. Sweet paprika measures 0–100 SHU. Modern practice uses HPLC ASTA pungency units (1 unit ≈ 15 SHU). Conversion factor varies by method. | HPLC, Sensory panel |

## Category 2: Quality Control

Terms related to testing, inspection, and quality assurance procedures in paprika production and trade.

| Term | Definition | Key Standard |
|:-----|:-----------|:-------------|
| [Acceptance Criteria](quality-control/acceptance-criteria.md) | Pre-defined specification limits that determine whether a lot is accepted or rejected upon inspection. Typically defined in the purchase contract. May include AQL (Acceptable Quality Level) for defect sampling. | Contract-specific, AQL per ISO 2859 |
| [Batch Number](quality-control/batch-number.md) | Unique alphanumeric identifier assigned to each production lot for full traceability. Format follows internal SOP: YYMMDD-[origin code]-[sequence number]. Enables full forward and backward traceability. | Internal SOP per EU 178/2002 |
| [Grade](quality-control/grade.md) | Quality classification based primarily on ASTA color value. Four-tier system: Premium (160–200), Superior (120–160), Standard (80–120), Special (200+). Each grade also has corresponding limits for other parameters. | ASTA / ESA minimum standards |
| [Heavy Metals](quality-control/heavy-metals.md) | Trace element contaminants — lead (Pb), cadmium (Cd), arsenic (As), mercury (Hg) — with regulatory limits varying by market (EU, US, CN, JP). Analyzed by ICP-MS after microwave digestion. | ICP-MS per AOAC 2015.01 |
| [Microbiology](quality-control/microbiology.md) | Microbiological safety parameters: APC, Yeast & Mold, Salmonella, E. coli, Bacillus cereus, Listeria monocytogenes. Limits vary by destination market and buyer specification. | ISO 4833, ISO 21527, FDA BAM |
| [Specification](quality-control/specification.md) | A formal document defining all quality parameters for a product, including ASTA, moisture, particle size, ash content, microbiological limits, heavy metals, and pesticide residues. Forms the basis for the contractual quality agreement. | Contractual document per ASTM |

## Category 3: Processing

Terms describing paprika processing methods and unit operations from raw pepper to finished product.

| Term | Definition | Key Standard |
|:-----|:-----------|:-------------|
| [Blending](processing/blending.md) | Mixing paprika lots from different origins or batches to achieve target specification. Common for color standardization and cost optimization. Blending calculations use weighted averages of ASTA values. | Internal SOP |
| [Drying](processing/drying.md) | Moisture reduction from harvested peppers (80%+ moisture) to final product (< 12%). Methods: sun drying (3–7 days), hot air drying (12–24 hours), freeze drying (best quality, highest cost). | Temperature control per HACCP |
| [Grinding](processing/grinding.md) | Mechanical size reduction of dried peppers to target particle size. Mill types: hammer mill (coarse), pin mill (fine), cryogenic mill (ultra-fine, preserves color). Mill type affects color yield and heat generation. | ASTM E11 mesh standards |
| [Packaging](processing/packaging.md) | Protective packaging for export: poly-lined jumbo bags (500–1000 kg) for bulk, multiwall paper bags (25 kg) for retail/industrial, or vacuum-sealed blocks for premium preservation. | Food-grade per EU 1935/2004 |
| [Sieving](processing/sieving.md) | Particle size classification through vibrating screens with multiple mesh decks. Separates oversize and fines fractions. Sieve analysis performed per ISO 2591-1. | ISO 3310, ASTM E11 |
| [Sterilization](processing/sterilization.md) | Microbial reduction treatments: steam sterilization (121°C, 15 min), ethylene oxide (EO, not EU-permitted), or gamma irradiation (5–10 kGy). Method depends on destination market regulations and buyer preference. | USP / EP / EU reg |

## Category 4: Sourcing & Trade

Terms covering procurement, logistics, and international trade for paprika.

| Term | Definition | Key Standard |
|:-----|:-----------|:-------------|
| [Container Loading](sourcing-trade/container-loading.md) | Procedures for loading paprika into shipping containers with proper ventilation, moisture barriers (container liner), temperature monitoring (data loggers), and loading pattern optimization. | Internal SOP per IMO/ILO |
| [Harvest Season](sourcing-trade/harvest-season.md) | Annual harvest windows vary by origin: Xinjiang (Aug–Oct), Hungary (Sep–Oct), Spain (Aug–Nov), Serbia (Sep–Oct), India (Jan–Mar). Season timing affects freshness and availability. | Regional agricultural calendars |
| [Incoterms](sourcing-trade/incoterms.md) | International Commercial Terms defining seller/buyer responsibilities. Common for spice trade: FOB (most common for origin-shipment), CIF (seller arranges shipping), CFR (seller pays freight, buyer insures), DAP (door delivery). | ICC Incoterms 2020 |
| [Origin Traceability](sourcing-trade/origin-traceability.md) | Full supply chain documentation from farm plot to final delivery. Includes lot tracking, COA linkage, supplier records, and chain-of-custody documentation. Required by EU regulation 178/2002. | EU Reg. 178/2002, FSMA |
| [Supplier Qualification](sourcing-trade/supplier-qualification.md) | Formal evaluation process including questionnaires, documentation review, on-site audits, sample evaluation, and ongoing performance scoring. | Internal SOP per GFSI |

## Category 5: Documentation & Certification

Terms related to compliance documents and certification systems.

| Term | Definition | Key Standard |
|:-----|:-----------|:-------------|
| [COA (Certificate of Analysis)](documentation-certification/coa.md) | Document issued per lot showing test results for all specification parameters. Required for every shipment. Includes batch number, test date, results, methods used, and authorized signature. | ISO / ASTM test methods |
| [HACCP](documentation-certification/haccp.md) | Hazard Analysis Critical Control Point — systematic preventive approach to food safety. Seven principles: hazard analysis, CCP identification, critical limits, monitoring, corrective actions, verification, record-keeping. | Codex Alimentarius |
| [Kosher](documentation-certification/kosher.md) | Religious dietary certification ensuring compliance with Jewish dietary laws (kashrut). For paprika, ensures no cross-contamination with non-kosher products. Certified by recognized Orthodox Kosher agency. | Kosher agency standards |
| [Organic](documentation-certification/organic.md) | Certification that products are produced without synthetic pesticides, fertilizers, or GMOs. USDA NOP for US market, EU Organic Regulation for EU market. Requires full chain of custody documentation. | USDA NOP, EU 2018/848 |
| [SDS (Safety Data Sheet)](documentation-certification/sds.md) | Document listing hazard information, handling precautions, and regulatory data for paprika powder. Required for chemical classification and transportation. Paprika dust is classified as combustible. | GHS / REACH / OSHA |
| [TDS (Technical Data Sheet)](documentation-certification/tds.md) | Product-specific technical information including physical properties, nutritional data, and application guidelines. Supplementary to COA. | Internal specification format |
