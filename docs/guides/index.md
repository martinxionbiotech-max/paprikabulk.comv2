# Guides — Practical Technical Procedures

This section contains step-by-step procedural guides for paprika quality control, laboratory analysis, and supply chain management. Each guide is written for practical implementation by QC personnel, inspectors, and procurement teams at all experience levels.

## Why Use Standardized Procedures

Standardized procedures ensure consistency, reproducibility, and defensibility of quality decisions:

| Benefit | Description |
|:--------|:------------|
| **Consistency** | Same method produces same results across operators and time |
| **Defensibility** | Following recognized standards strengthens dispute resolution |
| **Efficiency** | Clear instructions reduce errors and rework |
| **Training** | New personnel can quickly learn established methods |
| **Audit readiness** | Documented procedures satisfy auditor requirements |

## Guide Structure

Each guide follows a standardized format designed for practical use:

1. **Purpose** — What the procedure achieves and why it matters
2. **Introduction** — Context, when the procedure is triggered, applicable standards
3. **Scope** — Applicable products, departments, and situations (and what's excluded)
4. **Equipment & Materials** — Required tools with specifications and alternatives
5. **Procedure** — Step-by-step instructions with critical timing, temperatures, and quantities
6. **Critical Control Points** — Steps where errors most commonly occur, with controls
7. **Acceptance Criteria** — Pass/fail thresholds and out-of-specification actions
8. **Recording & Documentation** — Templates and record requirements
9. **References** — Applicable standards and regulations

## Available Guides

| Guide | Purpose | Key Standard | Read Time | Equipment Needed |
|:------|:--------|:-------------|:---------:|:-----------------|
| [Sampling Procedure (ISO 948)](../quality-control/sampling-procedure.md) | Representative sampling protocol for paprika lots to ensure test results accurately represent the entire shipment. Covers sample size calculation based on lot size, sampling tools (thief/probe), lot segmentation into sub-lots, and composite sample preparation. Critical for reliable laboratory results. | ISO 948 | 10 min | Grain probe, sample bags, divider |
| [Sensory Evaluation](../quality-control/sensory-evaluation.md) | Standardized organoleptic assessment procedure per ISO 4121. Evaluates visual appearance (color uniformity, absence of defects, caking), aroma (freshness, off-notes, mustiness), and mouthfeel (grittiness, texture, solubility). Requires trained panel of minimum 3 assessors. | ISO 4121 | 8 min | Neutral containers, light box, reference samples |
| [Sieve Analysis](../quality-control/sieve-analysis.md) | Particle size distribution measurement using stacked sieve sets per ASTM E11 / ISO 3310. Covers sieve selection (appropriate mesh sizes for product type), shaking time (10 min on mechanical shaker), weighing procedure, and data recording. Particle size affects color release and dissolution rate. | ASTM E11, ISO 3310 | 10 min | Sieve set, mechanical shaker, analytical balance |
| [Incoming Inspection](../quality-control/incoming-inspection.md) | Receiving inspection checklist and SOP for incoming raw materials. Covers documentation verification (COA, packing list, shipping documents), visual inspection (container condition, labeling), container condition assessment (temperature, damage, pest evidence), sampling, and initial quality screening. | Internal SOP | 12 min | Inspection checklist, thermometer, flashlight |
| [Container Loading Inspection](../quality-control/container-loading-inspection.md) | Shipping container inspection protocol before loading. Covers container cleanliness (CSC plate check, interior inspection), odor check (no chemical or mold smells), moisture barrier installation (polyethylene liner), loading pattern (weight distribution, bag stacking), temperature logging (data logger placement), and sealing procedures (tamper-evident seal). | Internal SOP | 10 min | Inspection form, data logger, seal, flashlight |

- Customer feedback from audits and inspections

- Personnel feedback on practical usability

## How to Implement These Guides

1. **Download the guide** from the table above
2. **Review the required equipment** — verify availability and calibration status before starting
3. **Train relevant personnel** — ensure all operators understand the procedure through hands-on training
4. **Conduct a validation run** — perform the procedure with supervision on the first application
5. **Use the record templates** — each guide includes documentation templates for traceability
6. **Audit compliance** — periodically verify that procedures are being followed correctly
7. **Provide feedback** — suggest improvements based on practical experience
