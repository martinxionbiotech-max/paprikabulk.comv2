# Codex Standard for Paprika — CXS 242-2003

## Overview

Codex Alimentarius Standard **CXS 242-2003** "Standard for Paprika" (formerly Codex Stan 242-2003, adopted in 2003 and amended 2011, 2015, 2019, 2022) defines the internationally agreed-upon identity, quality, and labeling requirements for paprika traded across international borders. It is developed and maintained by the Codex Alimentarius Commission, a joint body of the **Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO)** and the **World Health Organization (WHO)** of the United Nations.

As the benchmark standard under the **WTO Agreement on the Application of Sanitary and Phytosanitary Measures (SPS Agreement)**, CXS 242-2003 serves as the reference point for resolving trade disputes regarding paprika quality and safety. WTO member nations are encouraged to base their national regulations on Codex standards; deviations must be scientifically justified under SPS provisions.

## Technical Background

### Scope and Product Definition

CXS 242-2003 applies to paprika (*Capsicum annuum* L.) in the following forms:

| Product Form | Definition | Key Characteristics |
|:------------:|------------|---------------------|
| **Whole dried pods** | Mature, dried fruits of *Capsicum annuum* L. | Intact calyx, pedicel may be present or absent; moisture ≤ 12% |
| **Crushed/ground paprika** | Dried paprika fruit broken or comminuted to particle sizes between 0.5 mm and 5.0 mm | Visible fragments; moisture ≤ 12% |
| **Paprika powder** | Finely comminuted dried paprika, ≥ 95% passing a 0.5 mm (US 30 mesh) sieve | Homogeneous powder; moisture ≤ 12% |
| **Paprika oleoresin** | Solvent-extracted or mechanically expressed concentrate (added in 2019 amendment) | Standardized to defined color and/or pungency |

**Exclusions**: The standard does NOT apply to:
- *Capsicum* species other than *C. annuum* (e.g., *C. frutescens* for Tabasco, *C. chinense* for Habanero)
- Fresh (non-dried) paprika fruits
- Paprika used exclusively as a raw material for oleoresin extraction (separate Codex standards apply)
- Processed products containing paprika as an ingredient (e.g., seasoning blends)

### Definitions (per CXS 242-2003)

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Defectives** | Pods or particles affected by insects, mold, decay, or other deterioration rendering them unfit for consumption |
| **Extraneous vegetable matter** | Edible plant-derived material other than paprika (stems, leaves, calyces) |
| **Extraneous mineral matter** | Non-edible mineral contamination (sand, earth, stones, metal fragments) |
| **Foreign matter** | Any non-paprika, non-edible material (plastic, glass, insects, rodent hair, etc.) |
| **Pungency** | The heat sensation produced by capsaicinoids; declared as "sweet" or "pungent" per national regulations |

## Key Provisions

### Quality Requirements (CXS 242-2003 — Section 3)

| Requirement | Specification | Tolerance / Notes | Test Method Reference |
|-------------|:-------------:|-------------------|:--------------------:|
| Moisture content | ≤ 12.0% m/m (powder, crushed) | ≤ 14.0% for whole pods | ISO 939 / AOAC 945.39 |
| Total ash | ≤ 10.0% m/m | NMT 10% on dry weight basis | ISO 928 / AOAC 923.03 |
| Acid-insoluble ash | ≤ 1.5% m/m | NMT 1.5% on dry weight basis | ISO 930 / AOAC 941.03 |
| Extraneous vegetable matter | ≤ 1.0% m/m | Max 1% by weight | ISO 927 |
| Extraneous mineral matter | ≤ 0.5% m/m | Max 0.5% by weight | ISO 927 |
| Foreign matter | ≤ 0.1% m/m | Strict limit — zero tolerance for hazardous objects | Visual inspection + metal detection |
| Defectives (whole pods) | ≤ 3.0% m/m | Combined insect, mold, and decay | Visual inspection |
| Volatile oil | ≥ 0.2 mL/100g | Minimum aroma quality | ISO 6571 / AOAC 962.17 |
| Pungency | Declared as sweet or pungent | Sweet: ≤ 0.3% capsaicin (≤ 500 SHU) | ISO 7543-1 / AOAC 995.14 |

### Contaminants (CXS 242-2003 — Section 4)

| Contaminant Class | Standard Reference | Key Limits for Paprika | Compliance Responsibility |
|:-----------------:|-------------------|:----------------------:|:------------------------:|
| **Heavy metals** | Codex General Standard for Contaminants and Toxins (CXS 193-1995) | Pb ≤ 0.5 mg/kg, Cd ≤ 0.2 mg/kg, As ≤ 0.5 mg/kg, Hg ≤ 0.1 mg/kg | Supplier — test per AOAC 2015.01 |
| **Pesticide residues** | Codex Pesticide Residues in Food (CXM 2-1993, updated annually) | Within Codex Maximum Residue Limits (MRLs) or national MRLs (whichever is stricter) | Supplier — multi-residue screening per AOAC 2020.01 |
| **Mycotoxins** | Codex General Standard for Contaminants (CXS 193-1995) | Aflatoxin B₁: ≤ 5 μg/kg; Total aflatoxins: ≤ 10 μg/kg; Ochratoxin A: ≤ 20 μg/kg (for spices) | Supplier — HPLC per EN 14123 or AOAC 2008.02 |
| **Radionuclides** | Codex GL 5-2006 (Guideline Levels for Radionuclides) | Compliant with national regulations; no specific Codex limit for spices | Supplier — gamma spectrometry |

### Hygiene (CXS 242-2003 — Section 5)

| Requirement | Specification | Recommended Methods |
|-------------|:-------------:|:------------------:|
| Production under hygienic conditions | Principles of HACCP and GMP (CAC/RCP 1-1969) | HACCP plan, GMP audit, ISO 22000 |
| *Salmonella* | Absent in 25g | ISO 6579-1, AOAC Official Method |
| *E. coli* | ≤ 10² CFU/g (n=5, c=2) | ISO 16649-2 |
| Yeasts and molds | ≤ 10⁴ CFU/g | ISO 21527-1/2 |
| Insect filth | ≤ 1% insect-damaged material | Visual inspection per ISO 927 |

### Sampling, Inspection, and Analysis (CXS 242-2003 — Section 6)

CXS 242-2003 references the **Codex General Guidelines on Sampling (CXG 50-2004)** for lot inspection:

| Sampling Element | Requirement | Rationale |
|-----------------|:-----------:|-----------|
| Lot definition | A quantity of paprika having uniform characteristics (same variety, grade, origin, production shift) | Ensures sample represents the whole lot |
| Sample size (powder) | Minimum 500 g from 5–20 incremental samples across the lot | Statistically significant representation |
| Sample size (whole pods) | Minimum 1,000 g from 5–20 incremental samples | Larger sample for defect assessment |
| Sample container | Airtight, light-protected, food-grade polypropylene or foil pouch | Prevents moisture/humidity changes |
| Retention sample | Minimum 200 g, sealed, stored at ≤ 20°C in dark for 6 months | Allows re-testing in case of dispute |
| Arbitration | Third-party accredited (ISO 17025) laboratory using specified Codex methods | Dispute resolution protocol |

### Labeling Requirements (CXS 242-2003 — Section 7)

| Labeling Element | Requirement | Codex Reference |
|-----------------|-------------|:---------------:|
| Product name | Must be "Paprika" with form designation (powder, crushed, whole pods) | CXS 242.7.1 |
| Pungency declaration | "Sweet" or "Pungent"/"Hot" must appear if the product is not clearly recognizable as such | CXS 242.7.2 |
| Country of origin | Must be declared for retail and bulk containers | CXS 242.7.3 — harmonized with CXS 1-1985 |
| Lot identification | Alphanumeric code traceable to production records | CXS 242.7.4 |
| Net contents | Weight in metric units (kg or g) | CXS 242.7.5 |
| Name and address | Packer, distributor, or importer identification | CXS 242.7.6 |
| Storage condition | "Store in a cool, dry place" or equivalent recommendation | CXS 242.7.7 |
| Date marking | Best-before or date of manufacture (national variations apply) | CXS 242.7.8 |
| Irradiation | Must be declared if treated with ionizing radiation | CODEX STAN 1-1985, Section 4.3.1 |
| Allergen declaration | Cross-contamination risk for shared processing (Codex allergen list) | CODEX STAN 1-1985, Section 4.2.1.4 |
| Organic claim | Permitted only if certified per Codex Guidelines for Organics | CXG 32-1999 |

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is Codex CXS 242-2003 mandatory for international trade?**
A: No. Codex standards are voluntary recommendations under the FAO/WHO framework. However, they become *de facto* mandatory under the WTO SPS Agreement: if a member nation imposes a more stringent requirement than Codex, it must provide scientific justification or risk a trade dispute. In practice, most bilateral spice trade contracts incorporate CXS 242-2003 by reference as the baseline quality standard.

**Q: Does CXS 242-2003 require ASTA color value testing?**
A: No. Codex does not specify a minimum ASTA color value. Unlike ESA standards (which require ASTA ≥ 100), Codex leaves color quality to contractual agreement between buyer and seller. This gap was deliberate — Codex accommodates the full range of paprika quality traded globally, from low-color industrial-grade (ASTA 40–80) to premium coloring material (ASTA 200+).

**Q: How does 2022 amendment affect the standard?**
A: The 2022 amendment (adopted at CAC45):
1. Added provisions for oleoresin (separate oleoresin product category)
2. Updated heavy metals limits (aligned Pb to 0.5 mg/kg for spices across CXS 193-1995)
3. Revised sampling plans (harmonized with CXG 50-2004)
4. Updated pesticide MRL references (2019 Codex MRL database version)
5. Added specific mycotoxin limits (aflatoxins, ochratoxin A)

**Q: Can a paprika product pass Codex but fail EU law requirements?**
A: Yes. EU law imposes additional requirements beyond Codex, particularly for pesticide MRLs (EU Reg. 396/2005 is generally stricter than Codex MRLs for 30–40% of active substances), mycotoxins (EU sets limits for spices that are 30–50% lower than Codex maximums), and nickel (EU has a specific limit of ≤ 0.5 mg/kg for spices via the food additive pathway). Exporters to the EU must comply with BOTH Codex and EU law, with the stricter of the two prevailing for any given parameter.

**Q: Does Codex require country-of-origin labeling for bulk shipments?**
A: Yes. CXS 242-2003 Section 7.3 requires country of origin on all product labels, including bulk containers destined for further processing. The intent is traceability for food safety purposes. Blended paprika (multi-origin) must declare all countries of origin with percentage composition of each. Example: "Product of India (55%), China (35%), Peru (10%)."
